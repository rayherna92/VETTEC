My Journey

  • The Beginning

    My journey starts all the way back in the year 1992. I was born in Los Angeles, California to a pair of young but wonderful parents. Shortly after celebrating my second birthday, my parents decided to move to Washington, D.C. I grew up on the eastern coast of the United States and have lived here for most of my life. During my school years, I have always been intrigued by math, science, and music. As such, I excelled in my math and sciences class, took computer programming and gaming design classes, and was part of the school bands (concert, jazz, and marching) since early as 6th grade.

  • Decision, Decisions, Decisions

    After graduating high school, my plan had always been to join the military. I was initially going to join the Air Force, but my father insisted on me going to college and so I decided to apply to a single school, the University of Maryland, Baltimore County. Surprisingly, I got accepted. I attended pursuing a degree in computer science. I did alright for my first year, but sadly quickly lost discipline in my studies and started to fail. In my sophomore year, I decided to drop-out and finally join the military. This time, with my older brother, we joined the US Navy.

  • The Journey So Far

    I completed 10 years of active service for the US Navy, and currently I am in the reserves. I do not regret my decision in joining, it probably has been the best for me. I was very immature and lacked self-discipline in the pursuit of my own goals. As challenging as the military can be, I learned many valuable lessons and got to experience some incredible things. Overall, the military gave me the opportunity to leave the starting line, well really it pushed me to finally leave. Through it, I have learned to overcome many obstacles of life, and I believe it has gained me strength, courage, and discipline to continue on pursuing even greater heights in life.

  • Introspection And The Future

    Today, I am back to my original starting line, but this is lap two. I have now gained the much needed qualities and experience I need to progress and really pursue something that interests me and can provide for an even better future. Not to say that there isn't more to learn in life, but I am now better equiped. All of this would not have been possible without the support of my family, friends, and mentors that I have met over the past 30 years. Now it's time to continue forward onto the next chapter of my life.
